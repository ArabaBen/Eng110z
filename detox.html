un: -pluging

by Araba

If we’re all being honest, there isn't much nuance to add to the conversations surrounding social media and mental health. Everything that was needed to be said has been said for a decade and some change now. The solution to many may be simple to most- like “just stay off the internet”, “delete your instagram, facebook, tiktok, whatever” it is you're doing that's causing and impacting you emotionally and mentally. But as stated it isn't as simple. For a generation that's so anxious and has grown up in the information age, asking or telling us to stop engaging with tech and media is like asking us to forget and walk away from everything we've known and done since youth.

In a sense, it's an unrealistic ask. Also considering how many of our generation's use of media paved the way for influencing careers and other micro or macro digital marketing careers. So for most, the ask of quitting media cold turkey is asking them to forgo their livelihoods and means of financial gains / independence.

We understand the value of using tech & media wisely because we do believe in the good of tech and media. Because with all the bad, there is good.

We have compiled a list to help you navigate ways to use tech and your media smarter and safely.

Privacy

• Think carefully about what you post online and who you want to see it. Consider setting your profiles to 'friends/followers' only.

Out of sight, out of mind

• Reorganize home screen. Try hiding your social media apps or place them in groups and on a screen page you rarely scroll to the moment you look at your phone.

Purpose

• Have a purpose and set good goals for your social media. Having purpose doesn't always have to be so profound. It can be just as simple, mundane, and silly as you want. If your niche is food content, your purpose can be as simple as “wanting to share food you love.” but also as silly as wanting to “make people hungry at night”. Therefore your goal should be to be active on your chosen platform for 10min to engage with followers/posts and to post your food continent in around a set time like 9pm.
• (+ not only will you feel good about your media use, but you’ll engage with things you care about all within a set time you made for yourself).


Do not disturb

Lockout times/apps exist to help you. Use them. Try apps that help you manage your social media and screen time. Some include:

• Forest allows you to set a period of time (say, 20 minutes) when you commit not to use your phone. During that time, a digital plant will grow in the app. If you use your phone at this time, Forest sends you a notification to get off the app or your plant will die! Daywise app
• Use your Do Not Disturb feature on devices to silence calls and app notifications (such as vibrations or the screen lighting up) & others.
• Silence all notification of your social media apps. scary , we know. But remember that notifications have been utilized by developers to keep you doped on that dinging noise. Free yourself.


Solo hours

• Dedicate an hour of a day each week to a hobby you picked up during quarantine , self care, dance break, etc. Just 1 hour for you. No phone, just you and your non social media hobby. You can do it.

Phoneless meals

• Ask your family for help. Add phoneless meal time to your family's routine. Start with one dinner a week where all phones and devices are away. You’ve got 7 days of 7 family dinners together. What will a day without your phone means 6 days of media use. It's doable.

Baby steps. We don’t expect perfection but we do hope you can build a habit of consistency.
Happy healing 😊
